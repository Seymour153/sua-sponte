Why targets, not floors

Floors tell you who gets in the door. Targets tell you who stays on his feet at hour 60. You already know this from varsity track — the kid running 4:20 miles didn't show up doing it; he trained where 4:40 felt honest-hard until it didn't. Same engine. Different event. Train toward the target; the floor comes free.

TIPP · Emergency Toolkit · DBT

Acute spikes only — panic, rage, flood. Physiological, not cognitive. They work on the body first.

T Temperature
Cold water on the face or a cold shower (30–60 sec). Triggers the mammalian dive reflex — heart rate drops, parasympathetic system engages. Fastest tool. Skip if you have heart conditions.
I Intense exercise
Burn adrenaline. 10–20 min of genuinely hard output — burpees, sprints, jump rope. Intensity, not duration.
P Paced breathing
Exhale longer than inhale (4 in / 6–8 out). Activates parasympathetic dominance. Different from box breathing — box is neutral focus; paced exhale is calming.
P Paired muscle relaxation
Tense a muscle group 5–7 sec, release on exhale, notice the contrast. Work through groups: hands → arms → shoulders → face → chest → abs → legs. 3–5 min.

DBT Skills · Quick Reference

Mindfulness — What skills

Observe — notice without reacting. Describe — put words to it, factually. Participate — enter the experience fully.

Mindfulness — How skills

Non-judgmentally — without good/bad labels. One-mindfully — one thing at a time. Effectively — do what works, not what's "right."

Wise Mind

The intersection of Reasonable Mind (logic, facts) and Emotion Mind (feelings, urgency). Decisions made from Wise Mind hold up over time. When unsure: sit with both, wait for the answer that feels BOTH clear AND true.

Distress Tolerance — TIPP

Temperature · Intense exercise · Paced breathing · Paired muscle relaxation. For crisis. Not a lifestyle — a pressure release.

Distress Tolerance — ACCEPTS

Activities · Contributing · Comparisons · Emotions (opposite) · Pushing away · Thoughts · Sensations. Distraction set when you need to ride out the peak.

Emotion Regulation — PLEASE

Treat Physical illness · balanced Lowering vulnerability / Eating · avoid mood-Altering substances · balance Sleep · Exercise. Every other skill works better on a regulated body.

Interpersonal — GIVE (maintain the relationship)

Gentle · Interested · Validate · Easy manner. When the relationship matters more than the ask.

Interpersonal — FAST (keep self-respect)

Fair · no Apologies for existing · Stick to values · Truthful. When the line matters more than approval.

Standards

Our targets — hard goals

  • 100 push-ups in 2 minutes
  • 100 sit-ups in 2 minutes
  • 20 chin-ups (dead-hang)
  • 2-mile run in 12:00
  • 5-mile run in 32:00
  • 12-mile ruck (47 lb) in 2:15

These are the numbers we train toward. The mind gets built at the pace that forces it to show up.

Ranger School floor — reference only

  • 49 push-ups / 59 sit-ups / 6 chin-ups
  • 5-mile run ≤ 40:00 · 12-mile ruck ≤ 3:00

RAP Week entry gates. Roughly 60% of all Ranger School failures happen here — not because the floor is high, but because most men haven't trained above it.

Steady state (what the target buys you)

  • ~20 hr training / day · ~3.5 hr sleep / night
  • ~2,200 cal / day against 6,000+ burn
  • 65–90 lb gear, carried
